# Wayline Higgsfield Opening Cinematic Brief

**Target:** 34–38 seconds, 16:9, 24 fps, 1920×1080 minimum  
**Method:** Generate seven separate 4–6 second clips and edit them together  
**Continuity rule:** Do not generate the cinematic until the final hero turnaround, splitstaff sheet, atlas-bracer close-up, and Valuehold/Decimara/Fracture environment references are approved  

## What to prepare first

Upload the same references to every shot that supports image conditioning:

1. Hero front, side, back, and three-quarter turnaround in neutral light.
2. Hero face close-up with neutral and determined expressions.
3. Splitstaff orthographic sheet in connected and collapsed states.
4. Atlas-bracer close-up, dark and activated.
5. One color/material board using the exact Wayline palette.
6. One reference frame for each of the first three worlds.

Use the same character/reference lock, seed, model, and style controls for every hero shot when Higgsfield exposes them. Generate at least four takes per shot. Never ask one long generation to preserve all continuity.

## Global style prompt

Append this to every shot prompt:

> Original stylized-realistic science-fantasy world built around celestial navigation and floating continents, believable human anatomy, practical layered travel armor, curved topographic metal plates, weathered limestone and bronze, one narrow luminous meridian-gold route line, lapis and storm-teal cloth, cinematic natural lighting, restrained energy effects, premium animated feature composition, clear silhouettes, physically believable materials, age-appropriate adventure, no text in frame, no logos.

## Global negative prompt

Append this whenever a negative field is available:

> copyrighted game characters, black silhouette fighter, ninja silhouette, mobile game UI, medieval castle cliché, generic neon cyberpunk, superhero armor, excessive spikes, skull armor, sexualized armor, blood, gore, killing, guns, photoreal celebrity, extra fingers, fused hands, changing face, changing costume, changing weapon, warped weapon, floating accessories, rubber limbs, camera jitter, flicker, strobing, smeared face, unreadable text, generated logo, watermark.

## Locked story clarity

The finished opening must answer three questions without a lore dump: what broke, why the
champions oppose the hero, and why both a duel and a Route Trial are required. Add these
three lines as recorded narration with matching editor-authored captions; if narration is
not available, use the captions alone:

1. `When the Meridian broke, every territory sealed its route.`
2. `To reach each seal, a Routekeeper must earn its champion's trust.`
3. `Win the duel. Read the route. Repair the world.`

Place line 1 over the beacon shutdown at the end of Shot 3, line 2 across the three guarded
routes in Shot 5, and line 3 as the bracer resolves in Shot 7. Do not generate these words
inside an image or video model. Captions use the real Atkinson Hyperlegible font, remain in
the title-safe lower fifth, and can be disabled independently of sound.

## Shot list

### Shot 1 — The line wakes, 0:00–0:04

**Purpose:** Establish the signature before explaining the world.

**Prompt:**

> Extreme macro in darkness: a hair-thin meridian-gold line ignites inside an engraved dark-metal atlas surface. The light travels steadily through curved topographic channels, revealing weathered bronze edges and tiny limestone dust. Camera glides just above the surface following the line, shallow depth of field, controlled quiet motion, the line is the only bright element. End as the line reaches the edge of frame and blooms into daylight. [GLOBAL STYLE]

**Edit:** Begin from black. Add a low metal resonance and one clean travelling tone. No title.

### Shot 2 — The Meridian world, 0:04–0:09

**Purpose:** Reveal scale and the connected-world premise.

**Prompt:**

> Match cut from the glowing line into a sweeping aerial reveal above clouds: several believable floating continents connected by enormous narrow celestial route bridges and distant beacon towers. The same meridian-gold line crosses the landscape from foreground to horizon. Sunlight, high-altitude wind, restrained cloud movement, camera cranes upward and slightly backward to reveal scale, no frantic movement. [GLOBAL STYLE]

**Edit:** Let the music open. Hold the final wide composition for at least 12 frames.

### Shot 3 — The break, 0:09–0:14

**Purpose:** Create the inciting incident without destruction or casualties.

**Prompt:**

> Wide view of a monumental sky bridge during normal travel. Engraved route coordinates begin rewriting themselves as thin gold lines slide into impossible paths. The main meridian flickers, splits into three misaligned tracks, and extinguishes toward the horizon. Bridge sections rotate safely apart rather than exploding; travelers stop and hold railings. Camera pushes toward the broken route while distant world beacons go dark one by one. No people fall. [GLOBAL STYLE]

**Edit:** Three beacon extinctions land on three distinct low impacts. Avoid an explosion sound.

### Shot 4 — The Routekeeper commits, 0:14–0:19

**Purpose:** Establish the player fantasy and hero continuity.

**Prompt:**

> Medium close-up of the exact referenced apprentice Routekeeper on the halted sky bridge. Wind pulls the short asymmetrical mantle after the torso, not before it. The hero looks from the broken bridge to the dark atlas-bracer, unfolds the exact referenced splitstaff with one clean mechanical movement, then plants it to steady a loose bridge panel. Camera begins on the bracer, arcs no more than twenty degrees to a determined three-quarter face, and settles. [GLOBAL STYLE]

**Animation notes:** Block bracer glance → decision hold → staff commitment → contact compression → mantle settle. Reject takes with sliding hands or a morphing staff.

### Shot 5 — Three routes, 0:19–0:26

**Purpose:** Preview the public demo and establish that each human champion guards a sealed
route rather than fighting the hero without cause.

Generate three separate 2–3 second clips and use sharp audio-led cuts.

**Valuehold prompt:**

> Side-on cinematic duel tableau on a sunlit wind-carved limestone survey bridge. The referenced hero with splitstaff faces a tall human Surveyor-General with a telescoping bronze spear. A visibly closed survey gate and dark route beacon stand behind the champion. Broken meridian line beneath the floor, strong readable silhouettes, cloth settles in the wind, neither attacks yet. Camera tracks laterally for one meter. [GLOBAL STYLE]

**Decimara prompt:**

> Side-on cinematic duel tableau in a storm-fed basin with reflective channels and stepped tide gates. The referenced hero faces a human Tide Marshal holding twin pivot sabers that visibly connect into a short glaive. A locked tide gate and dark route beacon stand behind the champion. Storm teal reflections, controlled rain, no attack, clear silhouettes. Camera moves slowly past a foreground route marker without hiding either fighter. [GLOBAL STYLE]

**Fracture prompt:**

> Side-on cinematic duel tableau on a stable platform among fractured floating limestone islands. The referenced hero faces a compact human Chain Warden holding a blunt crescent counterweight weapon with a segmented turquoise energy tether. A retracted causeway and dark route beacon stand behind the champion. Deep cloud void, oxide armor accents, drifting background islands, no attack, clear silhouettes. [GLOBAL STYLE]

### Shot 6 — First duel line, 0:26–0:32

**Purpose:** Promise animation quality and the original 2.5D combat format.

**Prompt:**

> Fully 3D side-on weapon duel on the Valuehold bridge, exact referenced hero and Surveyor-General. The boss shows a readable spear-thrust anticipation by shifting weight back and aligning the spear, brief hold, then commits in one fast line. The hero parries with the splitstaff, clear weapon contact, two-frame visual impact emphasis, sparks and limestone dust, both bodies follow through and recover into stable guards. Camera remains readable and nearly side-on with a subtle impact push, no spinning orbit. [GLOBAL STYLE]

**Animation notes:** Select the take from still frames first: anticipation, pre-contact, contact, recovery. Reject any take whose contact is hidden by particles.

### Shot 7 — Reconnection and title plate, 0:32–0:38

**Purpose:** Resolve the visual idea and create a clean editorial title moment.

**Prompt:**

> Close-up of the hero's atlas-bracer after the parry. Three precise non-text route marks align on the bracer to imply that the route has been read and verified; only then does one meridian-gold route line reconnect, travel off the bracer into the bridge, and race toward a distant beacon that relights above the clouds. Camera rises to a clean wide frame with the hero small at lower center and open negative sky in the upper middle for an editor-added title. Hold the final composition, no generated words or logo. [GLOBAL STYLE]

**Edit:** Add the real vector title `WAYLINE` and subtitle `THE BROKEN MERIDIAN` in post. Do not use generated lettering. End with one quiet route pulse, not a trailer boom.

## Music and sound

- Begin with glassy metal resonance and a single travelling tone.
- Add low strings and hand percussion only after the world reveal.
- Use route pulses as the rhythmic through-line.
- Weapon contact has one tight metal transient, staff resonance, short stone dust tail, and no wet impact.
- Dialogue is unnecessary. Provide captions only for the final title if the platform treats it as content.

## Editorial rules

- Cut on the travelling route line, beacon extinctions, weapon mechanism locks, and parry contact.
- Preserve at least one calm hold in shots 2, 4, and 7; constant motion looks synthetic and reduces scale.
- Apply one final color grade across every generated clip. Match skin tone, gold-line hue, black level, and cloud white point.
- Use optical flow only after checking hands, weapons, cloth edges, and gold-line continuity frame by frame.
- Do not hide face/weapon continuity errors under motion blur.

## Acceptance checklist

- The hero's face, hair, armor, mantle side, bracer arm, and weapon remain consistent.
- No extra fingers, morphing weapon, disappearing armor, or switching handedness.
- Every fighter is human and full color, never a black silhouette.
- No blood, injury detail, falling civilians, or lethal hit.
- The meridian line stays the same warm gold, width, and material logic.
- All text and logos are added in editing, not generated.
- The work contains no copyrighted-game or living-artist reference in prompts or source images.
- Keep original prompts, seeds/settings, generation dates, source images, output takes, licenses, and final edit project in the asset-provenance archive.
